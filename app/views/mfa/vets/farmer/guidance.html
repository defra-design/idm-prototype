Guidance

Farmers: get a funded annual health and welfare review for cattle, sheep and pigs

Find out what to expect from a review, check if you’re eligible, the funding you’ll get and how to apply.

As a farmer you can get funding for a vet or vet-led team to visit your farm to do a health and welfare review if you keep:

  • beef cattle
  • dairy cattle
  • sheep
  • pigs

You can have one review each year for one type of livestock. If you have more than one type of livestock, you must choose the one that you would like reviewed.

A health and welfare review will help you:

  • reduce endemic diseases and conditions
  • increase animal productivity
  • improve animal welfare

The review will not be an inspection or be used for compliance or monitoring. The review is optional.

You can choose the vet that you want to do the review.

How much funding you’ll get

You’ll get funding for each livestock type, not for each animal. You’ll get:

  • £522 for a beef cattle review
  • £372 for a dairy cattle review
  • £436 for a sheep review
  • £684 for a pig review

This funding will help you cover the costs for:

  • the vet
  • endemic diseases and conditions testing
  • your time

This funding will cover the endemic disease and condition testing costs and will contribute towards the costs for:

  • the vet’s time
  • the farmer’s time

Check you're eligible for a review

You must have a minimum number of animals on the date the vet will visit your farm to do the review. You must have at least:

  • 11 beef cattle
  • 11 dairy cattle
  • 21 sheep
  • 51 pigs

The livestock must be registered in England and you must be the registered keeper.

You must be eligible for the Basic Payment Scheme (BPS). To be eligible for BPS the farm or business must have at least:

  • 5 hectares (ha) of BPS eligible land
  • 5 BPS entitlements on 16 May 2022

Your farm or business must also have a Single Business Identifier (SBI).

You can apply for a review if you have also applied for lump sum payment to leave or retire from farming.

What to expect from a review

The review can take up to 3 hours of your time and the vet’s time. You can agree with the vet to spread it over several regular visits to the farm or do it all in one visit.

The vet will also discuss one or more of the following as part of the review:

  • health and welfare, including issues such as lameness, body conditioning or mobility scoring
  • biosecurity, including internal and external biosecurity recommendations
  • medicine usage, including antibiotics, vaccinations and how you can register and submit data on the Agriculture and Horticulture Development Board (AHDB) Medicine Hub

You do not need to cover all of these areas. The review is flexible, so you can focus on the most important areas in the 2 to 3 hours the vet visits you.

You should agree with the vet:

  • when they’ll carry out the review
  • what they’ll do
  • what you’ll discuss

Endemic disease and condition testing

The vet will test for:

  • bovine viral diarrhoea (BVD) in dairy and beef cattle
  • the effectiveness of worming treatments in sheep
  • porcine reproductive and respiratory syndrome virus (PRRS) in pigs

You can use these test results for other accreditation schemes you’re a member of. Y ou cannot use other accreditation scheme test results to apply for funding.

The vet will not test for other medical conditions or diseases as part of the review.

If you’re already testing for these diseases or conditions, you can arrange a review so that it fits with your schedule. For example, if you recently carried out testing, wait until the next time you need to do it to take advantage of the review.

To find out more about testing you can read the technical guidance on disease and endemic condition testing.

Written report

After the vet has completed the review, they’ll give you a written report. The report should explain:

  • endemic disease or condition testing results
  • other review findings - for example, biosecurity recommendations, medicine usage advice or suggested species-specific health and welfare actions
  • agreed achievable actions to address issues raised by the review or testing - likely to be 2 to 3 but can be more or less based on individual needs

Information the vet will record and share

The vet must record and share some information about the review with Defra. The vet will share with Defra:

  • date of farm visit
  • livestock type reviewed
  • number of animals reviewed
  • endemic disease or condition test results

The vet will also confirm with Defra that they’ve given you a written report. The written report you get from the vet will not be shared with Defra or used for site visits or other compliance checks.

Test result information will help Defra get a better understanding of the health and welfare of the national herds and flocks. The information:

  • will be anonymised and stored securely online
  • will not be used for site visits or other compliance checks

When you'll be paid

After the vet has recorded the review with Defra, you’ll get an email asking you to confirm the record is correct. Sign in to confirm the record and submit your claim.

You’ll receive a payment within 28 days of confirming the record.

It’s your responsibility to pay the vet when the review is complete. The vet’s invoice will include any testing costs.

Apply for a review

You need to apply to get a reference number to book a review with your vet.

To get a reference number you can apply online.

The reference number is valid for 12 months.

Get help with your application

If you cannot apply online or have a question about your application, contact the Rural Payments Agency.

If you have your booking reference number, have it with you when you call or include it in your email.

  • Telephone: 01900 508622
  • Monday to Friday, 8:30am to 5pm (except public holidays)
  • ruralpayments@defra.gov.uk
