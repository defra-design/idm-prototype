Guidance

Test for porcine reproductive and respiratory syndrome (PRRS) in pigs

Published 11 July 2022

Test type and sampling

Use an enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay (ELISA) or PRRS polymerase chain reaction (PCR) test.

A vet must do the sampling. They should sample up to 30 pigs.

Sequencing is not paid for as part of the review.

Follow detailed guidance on how to do pig sampling and testing on the Agriculture and Horticulture Development Board.

Test sample laboratory analysis

You must send the test sample for analysis to a laboratory that is one or both of the following:

  • ISO/IEC 17043 accredited
  • UKAS accredited (UK accredited) for an ELISA or PRRS PCR test

Find an approved laboratory to test for porcine reproductive and respiratory syndrome (PRRS) in pigs

Record and report test results

You must discuss the test results and any further action you may need to take with the farmer as part of the review. You must:

  • record the test results online after the review
  • include test results in the written report you give to the farmer with appropriate recommendations

The PRRS test result can be used for other accreditation schemes that the farmer is a member of.
