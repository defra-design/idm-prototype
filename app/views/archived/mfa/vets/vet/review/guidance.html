Guidance

Vets: carry out an annual health and welfare review

Find out about the annual health and welfare review and how vets should carry out a review.

  • Contents
  • How much funding a farmer will get
  • How to carry out a review
  • After the review
  • Information to record and share

Defra will pay farmers to have an annual vet-led health and welfare review if they keep beef or dairy cattle, sheep or pigs.

The aim of the review is to:

  • reduce endemic diseases and conditions
  • increase animal productivity
  • improve animal welfare

The review is not an inspection or audit and it is optional.

How much funding a farmer will get

Farmers will get funding for each livestock type, not for each animal. They’ll get:

  • £522 for a beef cattle review
  • £372 for a dairy cattle review
  • £436 for a sheep review
  • £684 for a pig review

This funding will help cover the costs for:

  • the farmer’s time
  • endemic diseases and conditions testing
  • your time

The funding amount for each type of livestock is different because:

  • testing may take longer for some types of livestock
  • some results cost more to analyse

How to carry out a review

The farmer will contact you to arrange a review and give you a booking reference number. You'll need this reference number to record information after the review.

You should agree with the farmer when you’ll carry out the review and what you’ll check. You can spread the review over several regular visits to the farm or do it in a single visit.

The review can take up to 3 hours of your time and the farmer’s time. It can be flexible, so you can focus on the most important things for the farm.

Some parts of the review are required and some are optional.

You must visit the farm and test for:

  • bovine viral diarrhoea (BVD) in dairy and beef cattle
  • the effectiveness of worming treatments in sheep
  • porcine reproductive and respiratory syndrome (PRRS) in pigs

You must discuss the test results and follow-up actions with the farmer. Work with the farmer to improve the health and productivity of their animals. Give advice where there are issues such as lameness, body conditioning or mobility scoring.

You do not need to test for other medical conditions or diseases.

Follow guidance on testing for priority endemic diseases and conditions.

Depending on the farm, you may choose to review:

  • biosecurity measures on the farm and suggest recommendations for improvements
  • farm medicines, including antibiotics, vaccinations and how to register and submit data on the Agriculture and Horticulture Development Board (AHDB) Medicine Hub

At the end of the review you must give the farmer a written report.

This will include agreed recommendations and actions you or the farmer can take to improve the health and welfare of the livestock - for example, a 5-point lameness plan.

After the review

After the review, you, or a member of your team, must record information about the review.

You can record information about a review online

Recording information takes around 5 minutes.

After you submit the information, the farmer can claim funding.

You should invoice the farmer using your normal process. Your invoice must include the costs for:

  • endemic diseases and conditions testing
  • your time

Information to record and share

You must share some information you’ve recorded with Defra. You need to confirm:

  • livestock type reviewed
  • number of animals reviewed
  • priority disease or endemic condition test results
  • you’ve given the farmer the written report - do not share the report itself

Test result information will help Defra get a better understanding of the health and welfare of the national herds and flocks.

The information:

  • will be anonymised and stored securely online
  • will not be used for site visits or other compliance checks
