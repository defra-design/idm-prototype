The Your Defra account service will put small files known as ‘cookies’ on to your computer.

These cookies are used to:

  • measure how you use the service so it can be improved based on your needs
  • remember the notifications you’ve seen so that we don’t show them to you again

Find out more about how to manage cookies.

How cookies are used on Your Defra Account

Measuring service usage

We use Google Analytics and Microsoft Application Insights software to collect information about how you use the service. We do this to help make sure the service is meeting the needs of its users and to help us make improvements.

Google Analytics and Microsoft Application Insights store information about:

  • the pages you visit
  • how long you spend on each page
  • how you got to the site
  • what you click on while you’re visiting the site
  • the device and browser you are using

We do not collect or store your personal information (for example your name or address) so this information can’t be used to identify who you are. We do not allow Google or Microsoft to use or share our analytics data.
